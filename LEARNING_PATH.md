## üìö Learning Modules

### Module 1: Biochemical Foundations

Understanding the biological systems that drugs interact with.

**Lesson 1: Essential Biomolecules**
- Carbohydrates, lipids, proteins, and nucleic acids
- Structure-function relationships
- Drug target identification

**Lesson 2: Proteins - Structure, Folding & Drug Targets**
- Primary, secondary, tertiary, quaternary structure
- Protein folding and misfolding diseases
- Druggable protein families (GPCRs, kinases, proteases)

**Lesson 3: Enzymes - Nature's Catalysts**
- Enzyme kinetics (Km, Vmax, kcat)
- Competitive vs non-competitive inhibition
- Enzyme families as drug targets

**Lesson 4: DNA & RNA - Information Storage**
- Central dogma: DNA ‚Üí RNA ‚Üí Protein
- Gene expression and regulation
- Nucleic acid-targeting drugs (antivirals, anticancer)

**Lesson 5: Neurotransmission & CNS Drugs**
- Synaptic transmission mechanisms
- Major neurotransmitter systems (dopamine, serotonin, GABA)
- CNS drug design challenges (BBB penetration)

---

### Module 2: Pharmacology & Drug Action

How drugs work and move through the body.

**Lesson 6: The Drug Discovery Process**
- Target identification & validation
- Hit discovery ‚Üí Lead optimization ‚Üí Clinical development
- Regulatory approval (FDA/EMA processes)
- Success rates and timelines (10-15 years, $1B+ cost)

**Key Concepts Covered:**
- ADMET properties (Absorption, Distribution, Metabolism, Excretion, Toxicity)
- Pharmacokinetics vs Pharmacodynamics
- Dose-response relationships
- Lipinski's Rule of Five
- Therapeutic window and safety margins

---

### Module 3: Computational Drug Discovery

Applying computational tools to drug development.

**Lesson 7: Cheminformatics & Molecular Representations**
- SMILES notation
- Molecular fingerprints
- Chemical space exploration
- Similarity searching

**Lesson 8: RDKit - Practical Molecular Computing**
- Molecular property calculations (LogP, MW, HBD/HBA)
- Substructure searching
- 2D/3D conformer generation
- Visualization techniques

**Lesson 9: AI/ML in Drug Discovery**
- Machine learning for QSAR (Quantitative Structure-Activity Relationships)
- Deep learning for molecular generation
- Predictive models for ADMET
- Applications: virtual screening, de novo design

**Lesson 10: Structure-Based Drug Design**
- Protein structure prediction (AlphaFold, homology modeling)
- Binding site identification
- Molecular docking (AutoDock Vina, Glide)
- Scoring functions and pose evaluation

**Lesson 11: Drug Metabolism & Toxicity Prediction**
- CYP450 enzyme interactions
- hERG toxicity prediction
- Ames mutagenicity testing
- In silico ADMET tools

---

### Module 4: Advanced Topics & Applications

Real-world case studies and emerging technologies.

**Lesson 12: Success Stories in Drug Discovery**
- Case study: Imatinib (Gleevec) - first targeted cancer therapy
- Case study: SARS-CoV-2 antivirals (Paxlovid development)
- Fragment-based drug design examples
- AI-discovered drug candidates in clinical trials
